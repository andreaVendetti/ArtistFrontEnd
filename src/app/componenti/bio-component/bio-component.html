Paolo Frattarola was born in Rome in 2005 and is currently a student at the Academy of Fine Arts in Rome.

His practice focuses on a search for images, "interior" or photographic, from which he extrapolates an emotional sensation, intrinsic to the subject of his representations. Images become a medium to try to show the very falsity of the image, consumed, ultra-visualized, preconceived, but constantly empty, meaningless. Last but not least is the role of the title, an attempt to call the subject-object a "work of art," conveying the nonsense of the represented image, on the level of the meaninglessness of words, language, and writing; playing on the probability of significance and the possibilities of interpretation.
